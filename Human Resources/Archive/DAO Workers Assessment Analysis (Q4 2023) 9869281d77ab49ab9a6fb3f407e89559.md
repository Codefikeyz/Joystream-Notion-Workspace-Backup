**3.1.0 Builders Working Group - 11/12 Responses**

Summary: The feedback from the Builders Working Group indicates dissatisfaction with current compensation and a strong emphasis on the need to increase salaries to support the group's ambitious goals. Addressing compensation issues is crucial to maintain motivation and commitment among workers and to ensure the successful achievement of the group's objectives.

Recommendation: It is essential to conduct a thorough review of the compensation structure to ensure it aligns with market rates and adequately recognizes the skill and dedication required for their roles. Additionally, fostering transparent communication to address compensation-related concerns and making workers feel valued is crucial. Moreover, acknowledging the financial implications of ambitious projects and allocating resources to cover infrastructure and development expenses is vital for the group's success and worker satisfaction within the DAO.

**3.1.1 Content Working Group - 6/6 Responses**
Summary: Feedback primarily emphasizes dissatisfaction with the current salary in relation to the workload. There's a consensus that increased work responsibilities and workload should correlate with better compensation.

Recomendation: There is a need for salary review which  aims to ensure that compensation accurately matches the workload and responsibilities carried out by each role in the WG. Additionally, a transparent compensation policy should be established, to provide clarity and understanding to workers regarding how their compensation is determined, particularly in cases where workload or responsibilities increase.

**3.1.2 Distribution Working Group - 4/7 Responses**
Summary: There is a concern for payments pegged to USDT which drops in value with the market volatility. 
Recommendation: A more structured adoption of the EMA30 $JOY price to allow workers to benefit from market upturns.

**3.1.3 Human Resources Working Group - 4/4 Responses**
Summary: The feedback from workers suggests a desire for a fair minimum wage or salary floor, increased compensation for long-term workers who helped build the project, and a more responsive compensation model that rewards additional responsibilities and acquired skills.

Recommendation: Implement a compensation structure that takes into account time invested in the role, skills and responsibilities, ensuring that workers are appropriately rewarded for their contributions and growth.

**3.1.4 Marketing Working Group - 10/10 Responses**

Summary: The feedback highlights several concerns related to compensation, and the practical challenges faced in their roles. The respondents generally feel that the current compensation structure does not adequately reflect the effort and challenges they encounter.

Recomendation: Implement a more flexible compensation structure that accounts for effort, time, and challenges faced by workers, particularly in roles with uncertain outcomes.

**3.2 Compensation Comparison**

![01ha.png](DAO%20Workers%20Assessment%20Analysis%20(Q4%202023)/01ha.png)

Summary: The general feedback from all workers indicates a broad concern regarding compensation, with a focus on perceived underpayment, misalignment with industry standards, and a desire for fair compensation that reflects their contributions.

Recommendation: Establish a transparent compensation model that considers both time spent and results achieved. Also a consideration to align salaries with industry standards and implementing a USD flat rate, and regular salary reviews to reflect the project's growth and evolving responsibilities are essential. This will ensure fair and equitable pay for DAO workers and is fundamental to addressing concerns about compensation and job satisfaction.

**3.3 Raise in Salary or Bonuses Received**

![1bonus.png](DAO%20Workers%20Assessment%20Analysis%20(Q4%202023)/1bonus.png)

The feedback suggests that some workers are receiving bonuses and raises in various forms, such as performance-based rewards, server cost coverage, and additional compensation for extra work while some do not. To motivate and retain workers, it's important to continue offering performance-related bonuses and raises, emphasizing their contribution to the DAO's success. Maintaining a transparent and consistent reward systems for exceptional performance can increase worker satisfaction and engagement in Joystream DAO.

**3.5 Work Resources**

![1resource.png](DAO%20Workers%20Assessment%20Analysis%20(Q4%202023)/1resource.png)

Summary: Workers (MWG outreach specialists) express concerns about not receiving adequate guidance or resources, particularly when convincing creators to join the DAO. Also, creators report their accounts out of fear of being hacked, leading to multiple loss of account.

Recommendation: Provide comprehensive training, guidelines, and resources to support workers in outreach to creators and addressing security concerns effectively.

**4.2 Workload Time Management**

Summary: There is concern for workers responding to creators outside regular work hours without fair compensation and requests more flexibility for special occasions. They request leads provide extra time when necessary, in accomplishing tasks.

Recommendation: Offering extra compensation for workers responding to creators outside regular hours after a careful assesment, to ensure fairness. Explore flexible working hours for special occasions and encourage leads to grant extra time when necessary.

**4.3 Healthy Work Life**

![1hwklife.png](DAO%20Workers%20Assessment%20Analysis%20(Q4%202023)/1hwklife.png)

Summary: Workers have been waiting for months for the Council to make a decision regarding salary increases, expressing the need for fair treatment and for their voices to be heard. Additionally, the absence of designated rest days in a 24/7 working environment is affecting the work-life balance.

Recommendation: Address salary adjustments promptly, using flat USD rate for all workers through the council EMA30 $JOY price budgeting, to recognize worker dedication and financial challenges, and establish designated rest days to enhance worker well-being and productivity.
