# **History and achievements of DAOs**

![](https://miro.medium.com/max/1136/1*adIwexvb3aqdyqJ6tlVDGQ.png)

DAO is a Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO), first launched in 2016 on the Ethereum blockchain. After raising $150 million worth of Ether (ETH) through a token sale. But after that, DAO was hacked due to vulnerabilities in its code base.

How did it happen?In early May 2016, several members of the Ethereum community announced the creation of The DAO, also known as the Genesis DAO. It was built as a smart contract on the Ethereum blockchain. The coding platform was developed open source by the Slock.It team, but it was deployed under the name ‚ÄúDAO‚Äù by members of the Ethereum community. The DAO had a creation period during which anyone could send ether to a unique wallet address in exchange for DAO tokens on a scale of 1 to 100. The creation period was an unexpected success as it managed to raise 12.7 million ether (at the time it cost about $150 million), making it the largest crowdfunding effort ever. At one point, when Ether was trading at $20, total Ether from the DAO was worth over $250 million.

But on June 17, 2016, a hacker found a loophole in the code that allowed him to withdraw funds from The DAO. In the first few hours of the attack, 3.6 million ETH was stolen, which at that time was equivalent to $70 million.

While traditional organizations are run by specific leaders, DAOs are run by computer code. DAOs obtain their consensus through digital contracts that exist on the blockchain. Virtual contracts are executed automatically and contain immutable data protected from unauthorized access. Algorithms integrate complex data and get rid of the requirements of human guidance. At the same time, making decisions through DAO is faster and more convenient.
