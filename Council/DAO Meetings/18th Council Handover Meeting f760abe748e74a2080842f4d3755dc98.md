## Meeting Note

**17th Council Summary:**

**Term Highlights:**

- During the 17th Council Term, significant progress was achieved.
- Out of the 8 proposals submitted by the Builders Working Group Lead, 6 were approved.
- The $JOY token value experienced a notable increase, reflecting positive market sentiment.
- The validator network was expanded, enhancing the stability and security of the Joystream platform.
- An in-depth discussion took place regarding the implementation and distribution of NFT rewards.
- The Query Node issue was proactively resolved
- The Council continued to consider the implementation of a server compensation plan to ensure fairness and sustainability.
- The term concluded with a total of 53 workers actively contributing to the DAO's objectives.

**Working Group Updates:**

**Distribution Working Group:**

- The Distribution Working Group identified data issues associated with distribution node offloads.
- Lead successfully completed comprehensive plans to revamp the Distribution system, aiming to enhance its efficiency and reliability.
- Efforts were made to explore cost-saving measures for operating distribution nodes, focusing on optimizing resource allocation.

**Builders Working Group:**

- The Builders Working Group introduced a revamped compensation plan, which required signal proposals for all tasks to ensure transparency and alignment with the community's priorities.
- Notable accomplishments during the term included the completion of tasks such as the Pioneer update and the preview of Pioneer mobile design.
- However, delays were encountered in the Validator module due to the late initiation of tasks and unexpected QN issues.
- The group is actively working on planning and preparations for Term 18 initiatives.

**HR Working Group:**

- The HR Working Group made significant strides in enhancing organizational effectiveness.
- A DAO Coordinator was hired
- Continuation of the weekly summary reports ensured transparent communication and accountability.
- Support in the YPP-support channel was bolstered, providing prompt assistance to community members.
- The progressive compilation of Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) aimed to improve community support.

**Marketing Working Group:**

- The Marketing Working Group remained proactive in promoting the Joystream platform and engaging the community.
- Research efforts continued in the development of an Ambassador program, which is poised to strengthen community relationships.
- Outreach activities included collaborations with YouTube content creators, expanding the platform's reach to a wider audience.
- Marketing efforts extended globally, with a particular focus on targeting various regions.
- A new marketing campaign titled "Polkapals" was prepared to raise awareness among the Polkadot community.
- Initiatives to explore token listings on top-tier cryptocurrency exchanges were pursued.
- Promotions across platforms such as Twitter Spaces and other social media channels were conducted to engage the community effectively.
- Collaborations with marketing agencies aimed to optimize marketing strategies and reach.

**Storage Working Group Update:**

- The Storage Working Group reported normal operations with capacity utilization.
- An upscaling proposal was submitted to enhance system performance and accommodate growing demands.
- An upgrade for the Query Node was successfully completed to improve functionality and reliability.

**Content Working Group Update:**

- The Content Working Group reported a slightly reduced number of new videos during the term.
- They implemented robust measures to identify and flag illegal content through the Gleev through YPP.
- Efforts were made in rewarding NFTs to promote user engagement.

**Recommendations from the 17th Term Council:**

- Urgent reevaluation of DAO salaries to align with the significant increase in the $JOY token's value, ensuring fair compensation for contributors.
- Striving for equitable allocation of council resources across working groups to maintain a balance.
- Initiate efforts to fulfill requirements for listing on reputable exchanges.
- Implement and promote the Ambassadorship program to strengthen community engagement.
- Intensify efforts to promote NFT rewards following proposal approval, attracting and rewarding platform users.
- Promote community staking as a means to enhance network security.
- Improve scaling strategies to accommodate a growing user base and ensure a seamless user experience.
- Enhancing the execution of OKRs to track and achieve organizational goals effectively.
- Initiating 4th-quarter OKR goals to ensure ongoing progress.
- Hiring of HR Lead and improving HR operations.
- Continue progress in Joystream cold wallet support, ensuring secure and user-friendly access to funds.
- Improve the APPs working group.

**Discussion**

**On Budgeting:**

- There was a discussion of whether salaries should be paid in USD value or continuous payment in $JOY.
- Yasir opted for basic salaries in $JOY with output-based compensations in USD. There was a discussion about stable coin payments for external services.
- The current council's budget plans were discussed, with differing opinions on evaluating worker rewards based on crypto prices.
- Salary evaluation intervals were debated, with Yasir proposing shorter periods and Martin emphasizing predictability.
- Concerns were raised about salary changes affecting the council treasury and inflation.
- Council member salaries and their calculation based on market value were discussed, with Chaos77 suggesting payment according to current $JOY value, pending further discussion.
- The difference between Council, Lead and worker salaries remained unanswered, to be addressed in future meetings or forum.

**End of Meeting**
