# YPP 2.0 Release Post

# Intro

We have set up Joystream as an open source platform for video streaming services with a view to support innovation in the content production and consumption, which has been long dominated by incumbent closed corporate Web 2.0 platforms, such as YouTube. The overarching problem breakdown and our solution is covered with much detail in our [lightpaper](https://www.joystream.org/lightpaper.pdf).

Just to recoup, from the onset, our strategic view was always to attract developers who can quickly ramp up innovative services on top of the core Joystream video distribution platform, without the need to invest into the infrastructure layer and fully focus on their business model and building community. In order to further reduce the upfront costs for creating such video streaming services weâ€™ve built Atlas, an open source video streaming App that works with the  Joystream platform â€œout of the boxâ€.

While Atlas codebase is available for anyone to operate or build upon, JS Genesis, the founding company behind Joystream, made a decision to operate a single instance of Atlas, under its own brand - Gleev. Gleev was deliberately selected to act as a â€œshowroomâ€ of the broader platform capabilities as well as test the innovative aspects of Web 3.0 video streaming, and its exclusive features: 
- Public ledger of video content ownership, comments and reactions 
- Member accounts and channels linked to blockchain wallets
- Unique monetisation features: NFTs, creator channel tokens, channel tipping and member-only (gated) content. 

At launch of Gleev, JS Genesis team has deliberately chosen to focus on the single, specific niche - the Crypto creators in pursuit of the three major benefits
- Leaving the opportunity for the developers community to occupy other content verticals
- Benefit from the more targeted content focus, allowing to serve more relevant content for its users and build an engaged community around specific area of interest
- Reducing the onboarding and adoption friction for the new users who are being introduced to the Web 3.0 concepts

So Gleev was launched as a video streaming app for crypto creators and Web3 enthusiasts. 

# Launching YouTube Partner Program

In order to attract viewers a video streaming platform needs the content of course. And convincing creators to add their videos to the new platform is not a small undertaking. To minimise the barrier of adding new content to Gleev, our team has built a YouTube Sync service which helps to add new videos from existing YT channels without any additional effort. The service simply required to sign up with the Google account and grant access to readonly scopes of YT API. 

But nothing comes for free in this world, and creators, used to monetising their content on YT via advertising needed something else, and wanted it right away to be interested in signing up. So while our unique Web3 monetisation features are still in the development pipeline, we found a way to cover for the temporary gap in the creatorsâ€™ value proposition and saturate our platform with content - that was with YouTube Partner Program. 

The YouTube Partner Program rewards *crypto* creators for signing up, referring other creators and syncing content from YouTube. This approach has quickly paid off and the number of channels signing up quickly grew from a handful per week to hundreds of new sign up daily! 

With that many sign ups, the range of content quality (as a proxy of effort invested into production) of different channels, reputation and the influence in the community varies quite substantially. We wanted to find a way to reflect this in the rewards scheme and to make it attractive for a wide range of creators, from professional teams to solo part-time creators, while keeping the program budgets reasonably managed for a sustainable longer term growth. Quite naturally, the program terms took the shape of the progressive scale of rewards based on the channel popularity, expressed in the number of subscribers on YouTube. 

This approach, while brings an understandable reward scale in theory, introduced a number of challenges and inefficiencies that tempered our growth. We were failing to compensate creators in-line with the added value their content brings to the Gleev users. Compensation *strictly* based on the YT subscribers and being on Crypto topic, does not provide the tools to prevent the program terms being exploited by channels with the â€œriggedâ€ subscribers and views purchased from click- and bot farms, while at the same time we did not reward enough the recently created channels which despite the amazing content quality have not amassed the subscriber base. 

A better way to distinguish between channels *quality* had to be found to optimise the population of the platform with â€œgreat to watchâ€ content at a reasonable cost and fuel the rapid growth without running out of gas too soon.

To get the YPP feature out of the door quickly, we reduced the scope of the project to absolute operational minimum hence leaving some of the nice-to-have features in the backlog. The program took off nicely, and creators really embraced it, sharing the feedback as they were signing up, referring their friends and syncing their content. 

A few improvements were identified as candidates for the upgraded release:

- Better explanation of the sync service integration access to YT channelâ€™s data
- More focus on the platform vision upfront - explaining the Web3 unique monetization tools which are in the product and development pipeline
- Enhanced programmatic updates on the verification process and participation status
- More granularity on the syncing service queue, individually calculated for each channel

But the most frequent feedback was about the possibility to invite / subscribe the creators which are producing content outside of Crypto and Web3.

# Expanding Beyond Crypto

Despite the marketing and sales efforts been focussed solely on the Crypto community, the word of mouth, creators promo videos and referrals activity resulted in the vast numbers of new channels that signed up being from all paths of life, and not just crypto. 

A few months in weâ€™ve accumulated a large user base of the non-crypto channels (in fact x5 larger than crypto community), willing to sign up and participate in the App. We were leaving these users suspended, and the list of accounts which would otherwise could contribute to the app growth was simply frozen in the list of suspended channels.

Gleev team has been consistently excluding non-crypto channels from the YPP rewards scheme and sync service, while staying consistent with our initial vision of leaving everything outside of Crypto space â€œfor grabsâ€ to the non JS Genesis teams to operate in, and there was one alternative.

Almost a year later after the launch of Gleev as solely crypto-focussed app we arrived to having an alternative, category-agnostic app launched by the Joystream DAO members - [l1.media](http://l1.media). The app operation for growth required more focus and effort than was available by the team operating it, which resulted in the auto-sync service along with some other updates was not on the cards for that app. The major fuel for growth of channels on l1.media were the Gleev-rejects. 

The gap in the experience started to get wider with time and at some point it became  apparent that l1.media app is falling short of demonstrating the traction sufficient to count it as a fully fledged occupant of the chosen content vertical, and Joystream DAO is too busy handling the infrastructure demand growth and internal processes to be able to effectively focus on managing a second app in the ecosystem.
